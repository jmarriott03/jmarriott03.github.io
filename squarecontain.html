Course details

This module is aimed at practitioners who work with individuals or families who have or are at risk of a genetic condition, in any setting. It builds on basic biological principles to enhance development of the practitioner’s skills for application to clinical work. Throughout the module, theoretical material is related to clinical issues such as risk assessment and genetic testing. It is suitable for those working as genetic counsellors and for other health professionals working with individuals or families affected by a genetic condition (for example, haemophilia, haemoglobinopathy, familial cancer, inherited cardiac conditions). You will be encouraged to relate the material to your own practice setting throughout the module.

As this module is offered by distance learning, you will need to be self-motivated and have access to the internet. However, a series of webcasts at regular intervals will be used to enable specific topics to be discussed in depth and enhance interaction between you and the module tutor. You will be encouraged to attend and contribute to live webinars but these will also be recorded and available for downloading later.

The aim of the module is to enable you to:

  • Develop an in-depth understanding of the structure and variations in genetic material.
  • Utilise knowledge of laboratory techniques for genetic testing to support your understanding of both processes and results of genetic testing.
  • Integrate scientific knowledge to enhance your communication with patients and other health professionals.
  • Enhance your ability to evaluate scientific reports.

At the end of the module you will be able to:

  • Develop an in-depth understanding of the structure, replication and variations in genetic material.
  • Understand the principles of heredity and apply these to clinical situations.
  • Apply knowledge of genetic variation to enhance your understanding of mechanisms that cause genetic disease/conditions.
  • Understand genetic test results.
  • Convey complex scientific principles related to heredity to patients in a way that facilitates understanding.
  • Explain issues and practical aspects of genetic testing in a way that facilitates informed consent.

The module can be used as an optional module towards the MSc Contemporary Healthcare (Health Genetics).

Accreditation

The module fulfils the criteria for an assessed genetic science course, as required by the Genetic Counsellor Registration Board in the UK.

To achieve a Postgraduate Certificate, you’ll need to earn 60 credits. You can achieve 60 credits from a combination of this module and one or two other genetics modules. You will be able to use your PgCert Contemporary Healthcare (Health Genetics) towards registration by the European Board of Medical Genetics.

Entry requirements

You will normally be a health professional and have a degree in a health related field.
