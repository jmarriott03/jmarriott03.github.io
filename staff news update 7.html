Changes to Student Gateway
Following a review of the Student Gateway and the departure of the Dean of students effective of the end of the Spring term, some changes are planned to where the various student services will report into. Professor Richard Stephenson (DVC) will look after Careers & Employability; Learning Support & Wellbeing; ISAS; Student Funding Unit; Complaints & Appeals; Gateway Hub & DAS Admin. Residence Life & Student Accommodation; Market Research & Evaluation (data intelligence); Timetabling; Data Support; and Switchboard will sit with John Wright (CIO). David Alder (CMO) will head up Student Recruitment & Admissions; Widening Participation; and Children’s University. Important synergies will remain between each of these three key student service areas under the revised reporting structure.

Call for University legal documents

Plymouth University in the news

25% increase for ‘quality’ research funding
The University’s success at securing £8 million in ‘quality-related funding’ for research from the Higher Education Funding Council for England represents the fourth biggest jump in the country of any university receiving in excess of £5million, and puts Plymouth in the top 40 for research income. Read the article in the Plymouth Herald

Plymouth leads European project to teach nurses about sustainability
A strategic EU-funded partnership to provide education and professional development on sustainability issues in nursing has been launched by a project team led by Plymouth University to look at sharing best practice across the continent. Read the full article in the Nursing Times

Guardian University Awards 2015
The University’s Peninsula Dental Social Enterprise has been named Social and Community Impact winner at the Guardian University Awards 2015. This is the second national accolade for the PDSE flagship education and community project after winning a Green Gown Award in November. The success also builds on the University’s Guardian win in 2013 for the eBooks project. Find out more from the Guardian newspaper coverage

Plymouth students win national business competition
The University has retained its title as the champion of student enterprise after winning the FLUX 500 competition. The Plymouth team made up of business and psychology students faced competition from 15 other universities. See the story as reported by the Western Morning News

International Business workshops
The Futures Entrepreneurship Centre is teaming up with Devon & Cornwall Business Council to host two international business workshops this month. The events will cover the impact of culture in business, the importance of second languages, different marketing styles around the world, and how to prepare for work with international businesses - specifically looking at the Chinese and Dutch markets. Register online for 15 April (China), 4pm to 6.30pm, Room 202, Mast House and 22 April (Netherlands), 4pm to 6.30pm, Room 108, Mast House. Any questions please contact Pauline Hands or Amber Strong.
